1. Overview

Relatch is a document intelligence tool. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, and how we handle it. We have built Relatch with a minimal data footprint by design. Authenticated users can upload documents which are processed to generate structured outputs intended for use as AI context. We aim to keep our data handling lightweight, reasonable, and transparent.

By using Relatch at app.relatch.online, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

2. What We Collect

2.1 Analytics and Behavioural Data

Relatch uses the following third-party analytics tools to understand how users interact with the product. This data is aggregated and does not personally identify you.

  • Google Analytics — collects page views, session duration, device type, and general geographic region.
  • Microsoft Clarity — records anonymised heatmaps and session replays to help us identify usability issues. Clarity may capture mouse movements and clicks but does not capture passwords or payment information.
  • Google Tag Manager — manages and deploys the analytics tags listed above. It does not independently collect data.

You can opt out of Google Analytics by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on. You can opt out of Microsoft Clarity by disabling cookies in your browser settings.

2.2 Account Information

To upload and process documents, you need an account. Authentication and session management are handled by Clerk. You can sign in using Google OAuth or GitHub OAuth. When you sign in through one of these providers, Relatch receives a basic identity profile (such as your name, email address, and provider user ID) so that we can associate your session with your account. We do not receive your OAuth password.

We use this account information solely to authenticate you, scope your uploads and outputs to your account, and provide essential account-related communication. We do not sell or rent account data.

2.3 Waitlist and Contact Submissions

Relatch uses Formspree for some contact and waitlist flows on the landing page. If you choose to submit your email address or a message through one of these forms, that submission is delivered via Formspree and used solely to follow up with you about Relatch. We will not send marketing emails unrelated to Relatch. You can request removal of your email at any time by contacting us.

2.4 Uploaded Documents

Documents you upload (PDF, CSV, XML, DOCX, scanned images, and similar formats) are processed client-side where possible. When server-side processing is required, your content is transmitted to third-party AI models solely for extraction and enrichment. We do not retain your uploaded files on our servers beyond what is reasonably needed to deliver the resulting output. We do not use your documents to train any AI model. You are responsible for the content you upload.

3. How Your Documents Are Processed

Relatch uses a multi-stage processing pipeline to extract and enrich content from your documents.

3.1 Text Extraction

  • For standard digital PDFs and documents, Relatch uses a built-in PDF.js parser with Engine 3 capability to extract text directly in the browser.
  • For scanned or image-based content that requires optical character recognition, Relatch calls Mistral OCR for intelligent character recognition extraction. If Mistral OCR is unavailable, Relatch falls back to OCR Cloud Space, which first applies an OCR layer to your content and then performs extraction.

3.2 AI Enrichment

After extraction, the text is sent to an AI model for enrichment and skill file generation:

  • For most documents, Relatch uses Gemini 2.0 Flash Lite (approximately 80% of requests). If this model fails or does not meet the internal quality threshold score, Relatch escalates to Gemini 2.5 Flash for heavier enrichment.
  • Relatch includes a document profiler. If your uploaded document is detected as complex or data-heavy, the profiler automatically selects Gemini 2.5 Flash from the start, skipping the lighter model to save time.
  • If both AI models are unavailable, Relatch activates a fallback protocol that uses strict internal templates and domain knowledge rules to produce a best-effort output without any external AI call.

Your document content is transmitted to Google's AI services for enrichment. Please review Google's Privacy Policy for details.

3.3 Smart Analyzer

Before any extraction or enrichment begins, Relatch's smart analyzer scans your upload for corrupted files, unsupported formats, and irrelevant document types. Files that do not pass this check are discarded immediately and never transmitted to any external service.

4. Cookies and Session Storage

Relatch uses cookies and similar browser storage for two purposes: (a) analytics, placed by Google Analytics and Microsoft Clarity as described above; and (b) authentication, where Clerk uses cookies and session storage to keep you signed in and to maintain your session securely. Authentication storage is essential to keeping accounts functional. Analytics cookies are non-essential and you can manage or disable them through your browser settings.

6. Data Retention

Analytics data is retained by Google Analytics and Microsoft Clarity under their respective retention policies. Waitlist and contact-form submissions are retained by Formspree until you request removal.
